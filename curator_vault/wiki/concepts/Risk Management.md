# Risk Management

The identification, assessment, and prioritization of risks followed by coordinated application of resources to minimize, monitor, and control the probability or impact of unfortunate events.

## Key Perspectives

### Howard Marks (The Most Important Thing)
- Risk control is more important than return generation
- "You can't predict, but you can prepare"
- The best investors are those who minimize losses in bad times
- Risk is not volatility; risk is permanent loss of capital

### Morgan Housel (The Psychology of Money)
- Financial outcomes are driven more by luck and risk than skill
- Risk is what you don't see coming
- Getting wealthy requires taking risks; staying wealthy requires humility
- Room for error (margin of safety) is essential

### Peter Lynch (One Up on Wall Street)
- "You don't lose money when stocks go down, you lose money when you sell"
- Understand what you own to manage risk
- Diversification reduces risk but also limits upside
