# Second-Level Thinking

Thinking that goes beyond first-level (simplistic, surface-level) thinking to consider implications, consequences, and what others are thinking.

## Key Perspectives

### Howard Marks (The Most Important Thing)
- First-level thinking: "The outlook is good, so I'll buy"
- Second-level thinking: "The outlook is good, but everyone thinks that, so the price is too high - I'll sell"
- The goal is to think differently and better than others
- Consensus thinking produces average results

### Charlie Munger (mentioned in multiple books)
- Invert, always invert
- Consider the second and third-order effects
- Understand incentives
- Use multiple mental models

## Core Principles

1. **Think beyond the obvious** - What's the second-order effect?
2. **Consider what others think** - What's already priced in?
3. **Invert the problem** - What would make this fail?
4. **Use multiple perspectives** - Different mental models
5. **Be contrarian but right** - Different thinking that is correct

## Applications

- Investing: Finding mispriced assets
- Business: Competitive strategy
- Decision-making: Avoiding cognitive biases
- Innovation: Seeing what others miss
