# Value Investing

An investment paradigm that involves buying securities that appear underpriced by some form of fundamental analysis.

## Key Perspectives

### Joel Greenblatt (The Little Book That Still Beats the Market)
- Magic Formula: High return on capital + Low P/E ratio
- Buy good companies at bargain prices
- Mechanical approach removes emotion
- Long-term patience is essential

### Peter Lynch (One Up on Wall Street)
- "Invest in what you know"
- Look for companies with strong fundamentals
- P/E ratio matters, but growth rate matters more
- Tenbaggers come from everyday observations

### Howard Marks (The Most Important Thing)
- "Well bought is half sold"
- Buy when others are despondently selling
- Price is what you pay; value is what you get
- Margin of safety is crucial

## Core Principles

1. **Intrinsic value** - Calculate what a business is worth
2. **Margin of safety** - Buy at significant discount to value
3. **Mr. Market metaphor** - Market is emotional, use it to your advantage
4. **Long-term holding** - Patience allows value to be realized
5. **Fundamental analysis** - Understand the business, not just the stock
