> "is prone to mistakes that can be taken advantage of. Where might errors come from? Let’s consider the assumptions that underlie the theory of efficient markets: • There are many investors hard at work. • They are intelligent, diligent, objective, motivated and well equipped. • They all have access to the available information, and their access is roughly equal. • They’re all open to buying, selling or shorting (i.e., betting against) every asset."

## Insight

The theory of efficient markets relies on several key assumptions about investors, including their intelligence, access to information, and willingness to buy or sell assets.
