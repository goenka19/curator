> ""But the mind is an attribute of the individual. There is no such thing as a collective brain.
There is no such thing as a collective thought. An agreement reached by a group of men is only a compromise or an average drawn upon many individual thoughts. It is a secondary consequence. The primary act--the process of reason--must be performed by each man alone. We can divide a meal among many men. We cannot digest it in a collective stomach."

## Insight

Individual thought and reason are primary and cannot be collectively performed, only compromised or averaged through group agreements.
